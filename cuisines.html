Grünkohl und Pinkel - Kale with Pinkel Sausage

A dish that is not necessarily everyone's fave, but definitely at the top of typical German winter dinners is Grünkohl und Pinkel. You can find a stand selling it at everychristmas market. And though I wasn't a fan of it as a child, by now I've gotten used to the very special taste and my mum even makes it at home occasionally.
It is easiest to just buy the sausages, but if you wanna give making them yourself a go check this our: https://www.essen-und-trinken.de/rezepte/44069-rzpt-gruenkohl-mit-pinkel
What you need:

  • 1kg kale
  • 300g of bacon
  • 40g oats
  • 400g onions
  • 50g resolidified butter
  • 4 Mettenden - Coarse smoked pork sausage
  • 500ml beefstock
  • 1 table spoons of sugar
  • salt and pepper

What to do:
  1. Place bacon in boiling water and let simmer for 30 minutes.
  2. Wash kale and cut stems off. Cook in salt water for 2 minutes, rinse with cold water after.
  3. Dice onions and steam in oil with the oats for 2-3 minutes.
  4. Add kale, salt, pepper, sugar and continue steaming for a couple of minutes.
  5. Then add the pork sausages, bacon and beefstock. Cover and bring to boil.
  6. Add the Pinkel now and cook on low heat for about 90 minutes.
And it's ready! It usually eaten by itself or with cooked potatoes that are roasted after and/or very lightly caramelized.

Grandma's Spaghetti

Probably the only dish on this list that is not exactly traditionally German, it is a traditional family dish. If you love pasta and tomato sauce or ketchup, this one's for you and it can feed you for days! What you need:

  • Spaghetti
  • Ketchup
  • Fleischwurst - (ring) bologna
  • one small onion
  • cream

What to do:
  1. Cut the onion and bologna and put it in a heated pan, stir occasionally.
  2. Cook Spaghetti according to the instructions on the packet
  3. Drain Spaghetti when done cooking and mix well with the onion and bologna and add the ketchup.
  4. At the very end add cream, stir and violá!
